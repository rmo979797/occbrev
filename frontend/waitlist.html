Waitlist Privacy Notice

Last updated: 15 June 2026 · Applies to the supplier waitlist only.

We're Occasions, a pre-launch event marketplace based in London, UK. This short notice explains what we collect when you join the supplier waitlist, why, and what you can do about it. When the marketplace launches you'll be asked to read and accept a full Privacy Policy before you can take bookings — this one only covers the waitlist.

What we collect

  • What you give us in the form: business name, category, service area, Instagram handle (optional), email, your answer to the open question, and whether you can onboard within 2 weeks.
  • Automatically, for spam protection: your IP address, your browser's user-agent string, and a timestamp of when you submitted.

That's it. We don't run any analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking on this page. See Cookies below for the one strictly-necessary cookie our security provider sets.

Why we collect it

  • To contact you when supplier onboarding opens (the only marketing email we'll send).
  • To prioritise categories & areas where we have the most interest.
  • To stop bots and abusive submissions (the IP + user-agent only).

Legal basis: your consent (UK GDPR Article 6(1)(a)). You can withdraw it at any time.

Who sees it

Only us and our service providers ("processors") below. We don't sell waitlist data, we don't share it with marketers, and we don't pass it to other suppliers.

  • Railway (US-based hosting, application server & managed PostgreSQL) — stores the row.
  • Cloudflare (CDN, DNS & email routing) — serves this page and handles inbound email to @occasions.london addresses.
  • Resend (EU-based transactional email) — sends your confirmation email and, later, your launch invitation.

Each processor is bound by a Data Processing Agreement that limits what they can do with the data to providing the service.

Cookies

This page sets no cookies of its own — no analytics, no advertising, no session cookie. The only cookie you may see is from Cloudflare, our security provider:

  • __cf_bm — Cloudflare bot-management cookie. Strictly necessary to distinguish humans from automated traffic and block abuse. Expires after 30 minutes of inactivity. No personal data is collected. Consent-exempt under UK PECR Regulation 6(4).

If you'd rather not receive it, you can block cookies for occasions.london in your browser settings — the waitlist form will still work.

How long we keep it

Until launch + 60 days. After that we either (a) migrate your row into a real supplier account with your explicit consent, or (b) delete it. If we decide not to launch in your area, we'll delete the row and email you to say so.

Your rights (UK GDPR)

  • Access — ask for a copy of what we hold on you.
  • Correction — fix anything that's wrong.
  • Deletion — we'll remove your row, no questions asked.
  • Withdraw consent — same as deletion for this waitlist.
  • Complaint — to the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk.

For any of the above, email privacy@occasions.london. We aim to reply within 5 working days.

Minors

The waitlist is for businesses. We don't knowingly collect personal data from under-18s. If you believe we have, email us and we'll remove it.

Changes to this notice

If anything material changes before launch we'll update the date at the top and email everyone on the waitlist. Reading this every time you visit is not required.
